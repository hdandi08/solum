Pre-Launch  ·  March 2026  ·  3 Reels  ·  Face to Camera
Context

What Is SOLUM?

SOLUM is a men's body care system. It's launching soon. The brand is built around one truth: most men shower every day and still have rough skin, a neglected back, and a scalp they've never properly cleaned. Not because they're lazy — because nobody ever built them a system worth following.

This content exists before the product is visible. The goal is to make men feel the gap — so that when SOLUM appears, it feels like the only logical conclusion.

Your Role

Who You Are On Camera

You are not a spokesperson. Not an influencer. You are an everyday man who started paying attention to something most men never think about — his body routine. You're sharing what you've found, like you'd tell a mate. No script feel. No selling. No brand enthusiasm.

Who he is Regular guy. Could be 25 or 40. Looks after himself but isn't obsessed with it.
Tone Direct. Slightly wry. Never preachy. Talks like a mate, not a presenter.
Never does Lecture. Hype. Use words like skincare, pamper, self-care, nourish.
Always does Keep it real. One idea per video. Land on a question or an open loop.
Tone Reference

How It Should Sound

✕ Wrong
"Hey guys, so today I want to talk about something really important for your skin health…"
✓ Right
"I've been showering every day for the last 15 years. Turns out I've been doing it wrong."
✕ Wrong
"Did you know that most men don't properly exfoliate?"
✓ Right
"Try reaching your own back right now. Go on. You can't. Nobody can."
The Content

3 Reels to Film

REEL 01
15 Years. Wrong.
Opening line — say this first, then continue "I've been showering every day for 15 years. Turns out I've been doing it wrong."
Script
Every morning — shower, cheap body wash, hot water, rinse. Done.
Skin still rough. Back always felt neglected. Scalp — I never once thought about my scalp.
I thought that was just... normal. Turns out it's not normal. It's just what we all do because nobody ever told us different.
We weren't taught a routine. We were taught to just wash.
Close on "That's about to change. Keep an eye out."
Location

Plain wall — bedroom, hallway, or kitchen. Not the bathroom. This is a reflective moment, not a shower moment. Clean background, neutral tones.

Wardrobe

Plain t-shirt. White, grey, or dark navy. No logos, no graphics. You're the guy watching the reel — dress like him.

Camera & Lighting

Face to camera, chest height. Natural window light to one side. Handheld or propped is fine — a slight angle feels real. No ring light.

Length  ~22 seconds
Props  None
✕ Do not mention any product, brand, or solution
REEL 02
Try It.
Opening line — then immediately do it on camera "Try reaching your own back right now. Go on."
Script
[ Attempts to reach own back — make it real, slightly awkward. That's the point. ]
Can't do it. Nobody can. And that's the largest skin surface on your body.
A loofah can't get there. Your hands can't cover it. So you just... rinse it and move on.
It's not laziness. The right tool just doesn't exist in most men's bathrooms.
Close on "Something's coming that fixes this. Watch this space."
Location

Bathroom. Clean, minimal — no clutter on surfaces. White or dark tiles preferred. The bathroom context makes the back-reach moment feel authentic.

Wardrobe

Shirtless is the stronger choice — the back-reach attempt reads more clearly without a shirt. Plain t-shirt also works if preferred.

Camera & Lighting

Wider frame than Reel 01 — waist up, so the back-reach moment is visible. Natural window light if available. Single lamp off to one side otherwise. No ring light.

Length  ~20 seconds
Props  None
✕ Do not mention any product or solution
REEL 03
Read the Label.
Opening line — hold the bottle while saying this "I checked what's actually in my body wash. Here's what I found."
Script
[ Holds up supermarket body wash — Lynx, Dove, or similar. Real product, not SOLUM. ]
Right here. "Sodium Lauryl Sulphate." That's a detergent. Same family as washing-up liquid.
And we're putting this on our skin. Every morning. For years.
It strips your skin barrier. Your skin spends the rest of the day trying to recover.
Most men have no idea. I didn't.
Close on "Someone's building something better. Keep an eye out."
Location

Plain wall or bathroom. The bottle is the visual — the background just needs to stay clean and uncluttered. Neutral tones.

Wardrobe

Plain t-shirt — same look as Reel 01. You're a guy who picked up a bottle and started reading, not a presenter.

Camera, Lighting & Props

Prop: one standard supermarket body wash (Lynx, Dove, etc.). When reading the label, angle the bottle slightly toward camera so the text is visible. Make sure there's enough light on the bottle — the "Sodium Lauryl Sulphate" line needs to be legible on screen. Face to camera otherwise, same framing as Reel 01.

Length  ~28 seconds
Props  1 supermarket body wash
✕ Do not mention SOLUM, amino acids, or any specific alternative product
General Rules

Consistent Across All Three

Lighting
  • Natural window light is best — raw and real
  • No ring light — looks too produced for this tone
  • No natural light: single lamp off to one side
Look
  • Same outfit or consistent look across all three
  • Plain t-shirt — no logos, no graphics
  • Reels 01 + 03 stay dressed; Reel 02 shirtless preferred
Takes
  • 3 takes minimum per reel
  • Take 1: exactly on script
  • Take 2: more casual, slightly off-script
  • Take 3: tighter, faster delivery
Delivery
  • Don't look at notes — know the idea, not the words
  • Direct to camera — not shifty, not intense
  • Slight pauses between lines feel real

Non-Negotiables

What Not to Do

  • Never say: skincare, pamper, self-care, nourish, cleanse (in a soft way)
  • Never mention SOLUM by name in Reels 01 or 02
  • Never show a solution — only the problem
  • Never look at notes or seem scripted
  • Never use influencer energy — no "guys", no "amazing", no forced enthusiasm
  • Never compare men to women
  • Never attack the brands you hold up as props — just read the label
He never has the answer. He only has the question.

These three reels exist to make men feel what's missing — before they see what's coming. The product is the answer. It arrives at launch.
