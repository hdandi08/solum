Copy Rules — What Research Confirms Converts

Applied to hero, product pages, email, and ads. Every line should pass these tests.

Do — Converts

  • Outcome headlines: "Properly clean for the first time." Names what changes.
  • Problem framing: "Most men have never had a proper back clean." Creates recognition.
  • System language: "The 10-minute routine most men never got taught." Positions as a gap.
  • Specific claims: "Apply within 3 minutes — skin absorbs 70% more." Concrete numbers.
  • De-risk language: "Skip or cancel anytime. One click. No penalty." Removes commitment fear.
  • Body confidence framing: "Skin that stays fed all day." Describes the outcome, not the product.
  • Ritual framing: "Muscle memory by week four." Makes the system feel achievable.

Don't — Damages Conversion

  • Shame language: "You smell" or "You have a hygiene problem." Closes people off.
  • Luxury signalling: "Elevate your grooming ritual." Signals high price, not value.
  • Vague benefits: "Feel your best every day." Forgettable. Converts nothing.
  • Fake social proof: "Marcus R., RITUAL subscriber" without verified review. UK ASA violation.
  • Fabricated claims: "99.9% antibacterial" unless you have test data. Legal risk.
  • Complexity above the fold: Showing all 8 products and 2 ritual tiers in the hero. Decision fatigue bounces mobile visitors.
  • Two competing hero CTAs: Splits attention, reduces primary CTA conversion.
