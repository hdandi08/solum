# SOLUM — Actor Shoot Brief
> Pre-Launch Content · March 2026
> One shoot day. 5 reels. 2–3 weeks of content.

---

## The Brand in One Paragraph

SOLUM is a men's body care system built around one truth: most men shower every day and still have rough skin, a neglected back, and a scalp they've never properly cleaned. Not because they're lazy — because nobody ever built them a system worth following. SOLUM is that system. It's launching soon. This content exists to make men feel the gap before they see the product.

---

## Your Role

You are not a spokesperson. You are not an influencer. You are an everyday man who started paying attention to something most men never think about — his body routine. You're curious, a bit surprised by what you've found, and sharing it like you'd tell a mate. No script feel. No selling. No brand enthusiasm.

**You are the audience.** You represent every man watching.

---

## The Character

| | |
|---|---|
| **Who he is** | Regular guy. Could be 25 or 40. Looks after himself but isn't obsessed with it. |
| **His discovery** | He started questioning whether his shower routine was actually doing anything. It wasn't. |
| **His tone** | Direct. Slightly wry. Never preachy. Talks like a mate, not a presenter. |
| **What he never does** | Lecture. Hype. Use words like skincare, pamper, self-care, nourish. |
| **What he always does** | Keep it real. One idea per video. Land on a question or an open loop. |

---

## Tone Reference

**Wrong delivery:**
> "Hey guys, so today I want to talk about something really important for your skin health…"

**Right delivery:**
> "I've been showering every day for the last 15 years. Turns out I've been doing it wrong."

**Wrong delivery:**
> "Did you know that most men don't properly exfoliate?"

**Right delivery:**
> "Try reaching your own back right now. Go on. You can't. Nobody can."

## The 5 Reels

---

### Reel 1 — The Opener
**Hook:** *"I've been showering every day for 15 years. I was doing it wrong."*

**What to cover:**
- He showered every day, thought that was enough
- Hot water + generic body wash + rinse = not a routine
- Skin was still rough, back always felt neglected, scalp never thought about
- He started asking why — nobody had ever built men a proper system

**Delivery notes:**
- This is the intro reel — relaxed, slightly self-deprecating
- Face to camera, conversational
- End on: *"Nobody ever taught us this. That's the problem."*

**Do not mention:** any product, any brand, any solution

**Length:** 20–30 seconds

---

### Reel 2 — The Back Problem
**Hook:** *"Try reaching your own back right now. Go on."*

**What to cover:**
- Physically demonstrate trying to reach your own back (on camera)
- A loofah can't get there. Your hands can't cover it. You just rinse it and move on.
- The back is the largest skin surface most men never properly clean
- It's not laziness — the right tool doesn't exist in most men's bathrooms

**Delivery notes:**
- This one has a physical moment — actually try to reach your back on camera, make it slightly awkward and real
- The awkwardness IS the point. It's relatable.
- End on: *"There has to be a better way."*

**Do not mention:** any product, any solution yet

**Length:** 20–25 seconds

---

### Reel 3 — The Ingredient Problem
**Hook:** *"I checked what's actually in my body wash. Here's what I found."*

**What to cover:**
- Hold up any standard supermarket body wash (Lynx, Dove, etc. — real product, not SOLUM)
- Read out "Sodium Lauryl Sulphate" or "Sodium Laureth Sulphate" from the label
- SLS is a detergent — it strips your skin barrier every single morning
- You're cleaning yourself with something that damages your skin daily
- Most men have no idea

**Delivery notes:**
- This is the most educational reel — keep it punchy, not lecture-y
- The supermarket bottle is a prop. He's not attacking the brand, just reading the label.
- End on: *"There's a better way to clean. Most men just don't know it exists yet."*

**Do not mention:** SOLUM, amino acids, or any specific alternative product

**Length:** 25–30 seconds

---

### Reel 4 — The Scalp
**Hook:** *"Your scalp is skin. I never once treated it like that."*

**What to cover:**
- The scalp gets shampooed — but never massaged, never properly stimulated
- Most men run shampoo through and rinse. That's it.
- The scalp has more sebaceous glands than almost anywhere on the body
- 2–3 minutes of proper scalp massage during shampoo — most men have never done it
- Simple change, immediate difference in how your scalp feels

**Delivery notes:**
- This is the most surprising insight for most men — play into that
- Can demonstrate on himself (hands on scalp, showing the motion)
- End on: *"Two minutes. I can't believe I never did this."*

**Do not mention:** the SOLUM scalp massager or any product

**Length:** 20–25 seconds

---

### Reel 5 — The Tease
**Hook:** *"I've spent the last few weeks figuring out what a proper body routine for men actually looks like."*

**What to cover:**
- He's been testing, researching, thinking about this
- Daily: 5 steps, 10 minutes
- Weekly: deeper ritual, 20 minutes
- Nobody has built this as a system for men — yet
- Something is coming

**Delivery notes:**
- This is the pre-launch tease — the first time anything beyond "the problem" is mentioned
- Keep it vague — "a system", "5 steps", "coming soon" — no product details
- Slightly more energy than the other reels — this one has resolution coming
- End on: *"Someone finally built it. Watch this space."*

**Do not show:** any product, any packaging, any SOLUM branding on screen

**Length:** 25–30 seconds

## Shoot Setup

### Location
- **Primary:** Bathroom — clean, minimal, no clutter. White or dark tiles preferred.
- **Secondary:** Plain wall, good natural light — for face-to-camera reels that don't need bathroom context
- Avoid: busy backgrounds, bright colours, anything that competes with the face

### Wardrobe
- Plain t-shirt or nothing (reels 2 + 4 work well shirtless in bathroom context)
- No logos, no graphics, no bright colours
- Keep it consistent across all reels — same outfit or same look throughout

### Props
- Reel 3 only: one standard supermarket body wash (Lynx, Dove, or similar)
- No other props needed
- No SOLUM products — they don't exist on camera yet

### Lighting
- Natural window light is best — raw and real
- No ring light — looks too produced, wrong for this tone
- If shooting indoors without natural light: single softbox or lamp, off to one side

## What NOT to Do

- Never say: skincare, pamper, self-care, nourish, cleanse (in a soft way)
- Never mention SOLUM by name in Reels 1–4
- Never show a solution — only the problem
- Never look at notes or seem scripted
- Never use influencer energy — no enthusiasm, no "guys", no "amazing"
- Never compare men to women
