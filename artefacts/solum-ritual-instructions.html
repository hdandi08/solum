How to do it
1
Step out of the shower. Towel off lightly — you want to remove dripping water but your skin should still feel warm and slightly damp.
2

3
Pump twice into your palm. Start at the legs — calves first.
4
Press, don't rub. Press both palms flat into the skin and hold for 2 seconds. Move up and repeat.

Work upward: thighs → stomach → chest → arms. One more pump for the second half.

The 3-minute window
This is not a slogan. The hydration gradient between warm skin and the lotion is what drives absorption. Let that window close and you've left the most important step half-done.

Mixing the clay
1
Scoop 2–3 tablespoons of clay into a bowl before you get in the shower.
2
Add warm water slowly — a small amount at a time. Stir between additions.
3
Target consistency: thick yoghurt — spreadable but holds its shape. If it runs off your spoon it's too thin; add more clay.
Applying the clay
4
Enter the shower and get warm — but do not rinse your body yet. You want warm, barely damp skin, not wet skin.
5
Apply clay with your hands from neck to feet. Chest, stomach, arms, legs. Use the exfoliating mitt to spread across your back.
6
Leave for 8–10 minutes. You will feel it tighten as it works. This is the clay drawing impurities out.
7
Do not let the clay dry out completely — if it cracks and goes white, mist with a little warm water from the shower head.
8
Rinse off thoroughly with warm water. Move directly to the exfoliation step — your skin is now fully prepared.
Atlas Mountain clay
Rhassoul clay has one of the highest ion exchange capacities of any clay — it binds to excess oil, toxins and dead cells and pulls them out as it dries. 8 minutes is the minimum for this process to complete.
