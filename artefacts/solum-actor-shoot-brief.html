The Brand

What is SOLUM?

SOLUM is a men's body care system built around one truth: most men shower every day and still have rough skin, a neglected back, and a scalp they've never properly cleaned. Not because they're lazy — because nobody ever built them a system worth following. SOLUM is that system. It's launching soon. This content exists to make men feel the gap before they see the product.

Your Role

Who You Are On Camera

You are not a spokesperson. You are not an influencer. You are an everyday man who started paying attention to something most men never think about — his body routine. You're curious, a bit surprised by what you've found, and sharing it like you'd tell a mate.

No script feel. No selling. No brand enthusiasm.

You are the audience. You represent every man watching.

Who he is Regular guy. Could be 25 or 40. Looks after himself but isn't obsessed with it.
His discovery He started questioning whether his shower routine was actually doing anything. It wasn't.
His tone Direct. Slightly wry. Never preachy. Talks like a mate, not a presenter.
Never does Lecture. Hype. Use words like skincare, pamper, self-care, nourish.
Always does Keep it real. One idea per video. Land on a question or an open loop.
Tone Reference

How It Should Sound

✕ Wrong
"Hey guys, so today I want to talk about something really important for your skin health…"
✓ Right
"I've been showering every day for the last 15 years. Turns out I've been doing it wrong."
✕ Wrong
"Did you know that most men don't properly exfoliate?"
✓ Right
"Try reaching your own back right now. Go on. You can't. Nobody can."
The Content

5 Reels to Shoot

REEL 01 · WEEK 1
The Opener
Opening line "I've been showering every day for 15 years. I was doing it wrong."
What to cover
  • He showered every day, thought that was enough
  • Hot water + generic body wash + rinse = not a routine
  • Skin still rough, back always neglected, scalp never thought about
  • He started asking why — nobody ever built men a proper system
Delivery notes
  • Intro reel — relaxed, slightly self-deprecating
  • Face to camera, conversational, bathroom or plain wall
End on "Nobody ever taught us this. That's the problem."
✕ Do not mention any product, brand, or solution
20–30 seconds
REEL 02 · WEEK 1
The Back Problem
Opening line "Try reaching your own back right now. Go on."
What to cover
  • Physically try to reach your back on camera — make it real
  • A loofah can't get there. Your hands can't cover it.
  • The back is the largest skin surface most men never properly clean
  • Not laziness — the right tool doesn't exist in most men's bathrooms
Delivery notes
  • The awkward physical moment IS the point — keep it real
  • Best shot in the bathroom, shirtless if comfortable
End on "There has to be a better way."
✕ Do not mention any product or solution
20–25 seconds
REEL 03 · WEEK 2
The Ingredient Problem
Opening line "I checked what's actually in my body wash. Here's what I found."
What to cover
  • Hold up any standard body wash — Lynx, Dove, etc. Real product, not SOLUM.
  • Read "Sodium Lauryl Sulphate" or "Sodium Laureth Sulphate" off the label
  • SLS is a detergent — it strips your skin barrier every single morning
  • Most men have no idea they're using a detergent on their skin daily
Delivery notes
  • Most educational reel — keep it punchy, not lecture-y
  • He's not attacking the brand, just reading the label
End on "There's a better way to clean. Most men just don't know it exists yet."
✕ Do not mention SOLUM, amino acids, or any specific alternative
25–30 seconds
REEL 04 · WEEK 2
The Scalp
Opening line "Your scalp is skin. I never once treated it like that."
What to cover
  • The scalp gets shampooed — but never massaged or properly stimulated
  • Most men run shampoo through and rinse. That's it.
  • The scalp has more sebaceous glands than almost anywhere on the body
  • 2–3 minutes of proper massage during shampoo — most men have never done it
Delivery notes
  • The most surprising insight — play into the genuine "why didn't I know this" feeling
  • Demonstrate on himself — hands on scalp, showing the circular motion
End on "Two minutes. I can't believe I never did this."
✕ Do not mention the scalp massager or any SOLUM product
20–25 seconds
REEL 05 · WEEK 3
The Tease
Opening line "I've spent the last few weeks figuring out what a proper body routine for men actually looks like."
What to cover
  • He's been testing, researching, thinking about this
  • Daily: 5 steps, 10 minutes. Weekly: deeper ritual, 20 minutes.
  • Nobody has built this as a system for men — yet
  • Something is coming
Delivery notes
  • Pre-launch tease — first time anything beyond "the problem" is mentioned
  • Keep it vague — "a system", "5 steps", "coming soon" — no product details
  • Slightly more energy — this one has resolution coming
End on "Someone finally built it. Watch this space."
✕ Do not show any product, packaging, or SOLUM branding on screen
25–30 seconds
Shoot Setup

Location, Wardrobe & Props

Location
  • Primary: bathroom — clean, minimal, white or dark tiles
  • Secondary: plain wall with natural light
  • Avoid: busy backgrounds, bright colours, clutter
Wardrobe
  • Plain t-shirt or nothing — Reels 2 + 4 work well shirtless
  • No logos, no graphics, no bright colours
  • Keep consistent across all reels
Props
  • Reel 3 only: one supermarket body wash (Lynx, Dove, etc.)
  • No other props needed
  • No SOLUM products on camera
Lighting
  • Natural window light — raw and real
  • No ring light — looks too produced
  • If no natural light: single softbox, off to one side
Technical Specs

Non-Negotiables

What Not to Do

  • Never say: skincare, pamper, self-care, nourish, cleanse (in a soft way)
  • Never mention SOLUM by name in Reels 1–4
  • Never show a solution — only the problem
  • Never look at notes or seem scripted
  • Never use influencer energy — no "guys", no "amazing", no forced enthusiasm
  • Never compare men to women
